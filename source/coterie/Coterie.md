==What is a coterie?==
Coteries form for a variety of reasons and while most do not form from a group of Kindred meeting for the first time at a bar, it's still possible. Formation of coteries can be done for a variety of reasons, being set up by another Kindred in order to achieve a specific goal, a group of mortals who once knew each other before the Embrace, or simply a group of Kindred with similar ideals and goals. Whatever the reason is, most Kindred know that while this group is likely to aid each other, entering a coterie does not ensure safety as they are still swimming with other sharks. All vampires still have their own desires and wants, exclusive of the group and it is rare for a coterie to know every member's pure and true intention. Despite all of this, coterie members also know that throwing one's allies under the bus is a fast track to adversaries and other problems.

Younger Kindred often describe coteries with a stylistic phrase. Such as a coterie of Brujah or Ravnos being a bande and Gangrel or Nosferatu being a burrow. Thin-bloods may not even know that there is a special word for this type of group. Many Kindred find the feeling of a coterie to be uneasy or unnatural, solitary predators don't often form social groups. However, there is an advantage to this, which is specific to the players having a sense of belonging. Whether a coterie takes down a path of betrayal or not, is up to them as the topic is easily traversed through other scenarios.<ref>[[Vampire: The Masquerade Players Guide]], page 154</ref>

==Changing coteries==
Vampires are social predators and fickle in how they align themselves, it is unlikely they remain within one coterie for their entire unlife. Because of this, many will change coteries multiple times or remain in more than one coterie at a time. Thin-bloods in particular will mix and match coteries as a survival tool to see the next night. Regardless of clan, this is a common attitude towards relationships in Kindred society meaning that claiming multiple coteries might open them up to increased scrutiny, it rarely does lead to banishment or violence. 

Whenever a Kindred does leave a coterie, any extra Advantage points may take a while to regain. If no character makes an attempt to regain the Advantage they spent on the coterie then the coterie keeps control of it. When they do make the effort to take it back, the coterie slowly loses access to these Advantages as determined by the Storyteller. <ref>[[Vampire: The Masquerade Players Guide]], page 169-170</ref>

==Dissolving coteries==
When a coterie agrees to dissolve their coterie, each member regains their Advantages much faster than if they left. If a member of the coterie was killed, those Advantages remain at the disposal of the coterie but no longer has protection. If these Advantages are harmed or otherwise lost, they can only be regained by spending experience from a different player character.<ref>[[Vampire: The Masquerade Players Guide]], page 170 </ref>
